Individual Sector Performance in the Fourth Quarter Of 2018

UTILITIES: The Utilities sector traded nearly flat with a return of 1.4%, extending its slight rise of 2.4% in the third quarter of 2018 for a combined return of 3.8% over both quarters. Ranked by performance, the components of the Utilities sector were Independent Power and Renewable Electricity Producers, Multi-Utilities and Electric Utilities with returns of 5.12%, 1.91% and 0.81%, respectively.
REAL ESTATE: The Real Estate sector fell slightly with a return of -3.8%, reversing its flat trading of 0.9% in the third quarter of 2018 for a combined return of -3.0% over both quarters. Ranked by performance, the components of the Real Estate sector were Equity Real Estate Investment Trusts (REITs) and Real Estate Management & Development with returns of -3.71% and -9.21%, respectively.
CONSUMER STAPLES: The Consumer Staples sector fell with a return of -5.2%, reversing its rise of 5.7% in the third quarter of 2018 for a combined return of 0.2% over both quarters. The top performers were Household Products and Beverages, with returns of 6.0%, -2.1%, respectively. The worst performers were Personal Products and Tobacco, returning -15.6%, -16.8%, respectively.
HEALTH CARE: The Health Care sector fell with a return of -8.7%, reversing its rise of 14.5% in the third quarter of 2018 for a combined return of 4.6% over both quarters. The top performers were Pharmaceuticals and Health Care Providers & Services, with returns of -4.2%, -10.3%, respectively. The worst performers were Biotechnology and Health Care Technology, returning -11.9%, -18.6%, respectively.
MATERIALS: The Materials sector fell with a return of -12.3%, reversing its flat trading of 0.4% in the third quarter of 2018 for a combined return of -12.0% over both quarters. Ranked by performance, the components of the Materials sector were Construction Materials, Metals & Mining, Chemicals and Containers & Packaging with returns of -8.45%, -11.13%, -12.23% and -15.18%, respectively.
FINANCIALS: The Financials sector fell with a return of -13.1%, reversing its slight rise of 4.4% in the third quarter of 2018 for a combined return of -9.3% over both quarters. The top performers were Diversified Financial Services and Insurance, with returns of -4.9%, -10.6%, respectively. The worst performers were Banks and Consumer Finance, returning -16.2%, -16.5%, respectively.
TELECOMMUNICATION SERVICES: The Telecommunication Services sector fell with a return of -13.2%, reversing its rise of 9.9% in the third quarter of 2018 for a combined return of -4.6% over both quarters. The Telecommunication Services sector had only a single component, Diversified Telecommunication Services, with the same -5.06% return as the sector.
CONSUMER DISCRETIONARY: The Consumer Discretionary sector fell sharply with a return of -16.4%, reversing its rise of 8.2% in the third quarter of 2018 for a combined return of -9.6% over both quarters. The top performers were Diversified Consumer Services and Hotels Restaurants & Leisure, with returns of -0.6%, -2.5%, respectively. The worst performers were Internet & Direct Marketing Retail and Leisure Products, returning -23.3%, -26.5%, respectively.
INDUSTRIALS: The Industrials sector fell sharply with a return of -17.3%, reversing its rise of 10.0% in the third quarter of 2018 for a combined return of -9.0% over both quarters. The top performers were Commercial Services & Supplies and Machinery, with returns of -6.0%, -13.2%, respectively. The worst performers were Trading Companies & Distributors and Construction & Engineering, returning -21.4%, -28.1%, respectively.
INFORMATION TECHNOLOGY: The Information Technology sector fell sharply with a return of -17.3%, reversing its rise of 8.8% in the third quarter of 2018 for a combined return of -10.1% over both quarters. The top performers were Communications Equipment and Software, with returns of -11.2%, -11.3%, respectively. The worst performers were Semiconductors & Semiconductor Equipment and Technology Hardware, Storage & Peripherals, returning -17.1%, -29.2%, respectively.
ENERGY: The Energy sector fell sharply with a return of -23.8%, reversing its flat trading of 0.6% in the third quarter of 2018 for a combined return of -23.3% over both quarters. Ranked by performance, the components of the Energy sector were Oil Gas & Consumable Fuels and Energy Equipment & Services with returns of -21.94% and -37.64%, respectively.
